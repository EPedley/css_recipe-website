INGREDIENTS
For the chicken:
  1. 450g chicken breast
  2. 1 clove garlic
  3. 2.5cm cube fresh ginger
  4. 10g shallot or onion
  5. 4 tsp light soy sauce
  6. 1 tsp ground coriander
  7. 1/2 tsp sugar
  8. 1/4 tsp chilli powder
For the peanut sauce:
  1. 100g roasted peanuts
  2. 2 cloves garlic
  3. 25g shallots or onions
  4. 2 tbsps vegetable oil
  5. 1/2 tsp chilli powder
  6. 1/2 tsp salt
  7. 2 tsps dark brown sugar
  8. Black pepper
  9. 4-5 tsps lime juice
Chicken Satay
This recipe is originally from Madhur Jaffrey’s “Far Eastern Cookery” book, published in 1991. It asks for the chicken to be barbecued over a grill, but an oven will work in a pinch too. The chicken is best left marinating for as long as possible.
For the chicken:
  1. Peel and crush the garlic, grate the ginger and finely chop the shallot.
  2. Combine the garlic, ginger, shallot, soy sauce, lime juice, coriander, sugar and chilli and mix well.
  3. Chop the chicken into bite-size pieces and cover in the marinade. Refrigerate for 2-24 hours.
  4. Grill the chicken in the oven at 180° for 20 mins, until cooked through.
For the peanut sauce:
  1. Grind the peanuts as finely as possible.
  2. Peel and finely chop the garlic and shallots.
  3. Add the oil to the pan and add the garlic and shallots when hot. Stir and fry for 1 min.
  4. Add 450ml water, the chilli, salt, sugar and ground peanuts.
  5. Stir and bring to the boil.
  6. Turn the heat to medium-low and simmer for 15-20 mins.
  7. Add black pepper and lime juice to taste.
